Task 1.1 the purpose of IT in modern business

IT has multiple purposes within modern business, ranging from the simple such as streamlining communication within the business as well as for both internal and external customers. This can be communication via E-Mail or as technology has evolved it could include live chat systems, online meeting tools, voice over internet protocol (VOIP) telephones and smartphones. Additional advantages of incorporating IT within a business include Inventory Management Systems which allow businesses to automatically track stock levels, and keep track of when new stock is required. There are also Data Management Systems which provide employees immediate access to files and documents which are stored on a business’ server. Management Information Systems (MIS) enable companies to track various elements related to the business; performance. Another aspect of IT which greatly benefits business are Customer Relationship Management (CRM) systems, which allow the business to capture every interaction with a customer in order to refine their practises and provide a more enriching experience for the customer. [1]


Task 1.2 Describe methods, skills and resources needed to complete projects successfully

There are a variety of methods, skills and resources which will assist you in completing projects successfully. An ability to communicate effectively and work collaboratively are two of the key skills required to complete any project successfully and efficiently. Alongside these skills the ability to problem solve with an attention to detail combined with strong organisational skills and the ability to coordinate with a team are intrinsic in any successful project. In regards to methods which contribute to a successful project I have come across two popular methodologies so far in my research; these are Waterfall methodologies and agile methodologies. Waterfall methodologies - These are a more traditional, straightforward and sequential method of completing a project, requiring one thing to be completed before another thing can be started Agile methodologies - This method embraces flexibility and collaboration to a much higher degree. The agile approach allows teams to respond to change quickly and efficiently. This allows the client to view the project at various stages of completion to ensure their expectations are being met before moving the project forward. [2] The resources required for a successful project are quite varied. They include the appropriate tools which can range from access to an appropriate compiler such as Visual Studio Code and a computer capable of handling the demands of developing the project, to external websites such as Moodlecloud, Stack, GitHub and Trello, which encourage and streamline your ability to work collaboratively. Other resources necessary for a successful project include a system specification from the client, which will establish their needs in regards to the completed project, and a budget. [3]

Task 1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects - what causes digital projects to fail? why?

One of the key risks with digital projects is the risk that the project will fail, according to my research between 70% and 90% of digital projects fail depending on the type of digital project in question. A significant contributor towards failed digital projects is a lack of active collaboration. However there are additional factors which contribute towards the failure of a project, I will address some of them here. Failing to account for business processes - This can be the most significant barrier to the success of a digital project. The problem tends to unfold as follows; Somebody notices a problem/inefficiency in how a company is delivering its product/service to the customer and proposes a digital solution that might help. They then work to push that through, selling the project as the next step in customer-centricity. However the tool/solution, while it does effectively solve the problem for the customer, doesn’t reflect or work well with the existing business process. Investing in cool gadgets not customer value - oftentimes projects that fail do so as a result of investing in a cool gadget but not investing in business or customer value. Another potential cause of failed projects is unnecessarily focusing on a newly learned technique over established best practice, which can lead to unexpected difficulties further down the development pipeline. The tools one uses and the methods employed should always derive from a clearly defined project brief built around a defined business requirement or customer need. Poor definition /Expectations setting - A poorly defined project will inevitably be afflicted with scope creep, leading to unrealistic goals and expectations, which leads to excessive content being included in order to meet the unrealistic goals not properly elaborated on and defined in the planning process. Poor vendor selection - The most common problem when it comes to failed digital projects. Poor vendor selection can jeopardise your project in a variety of ways beyond just their technical capabilities. Smaller clients of vendors may find their work deprioritised in favour of a larger client. On the opposite side of this problem is the selection of a vendor being under equipped to handle a client over a certain size or with specific requirements and thus struggling to service your account effectively. [4]

Task 1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects - How does GDPR impact digital projects?

The GDPR ( General Data Protection Regulation) is designed to protect the data of individuals, the regulation has a number of aims all designed to increase and standardise the protection of individuals including; Data portability - Individuals have a right to transfer their data from one service provider to another. And it must happen in a commonly used and machine readable format. The right to be forgotten - If consumers are no longer customers, or if they withdraw their consent from a company to use their personal data, then they have the right to have their data deleted. The right to prevent profiling - The right to object to processing, The right to rectification and erasure Subject access requests. Non-compliance with the GDPR can lead to the possibility of fines of 4% of turnover or up to $20 million or £16 million. [5] The GDPR outlines two important roles in the management and protection of data; Controller - An entity that decides the purpose and manner that data is or will be used. Processor - The person or group that processes the data on behalf of the controller. Processing is obtaining, recording, adapting or holding personal data. Any data collected must be collected and used for the purpose laid out by the Data controller and for no longer than was specified when the data collection was implemented. [6]
